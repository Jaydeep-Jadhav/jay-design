Home / Designing with AI

AI didn't replace my process. It sharpened where my judgment matters.

Most designers say they "use AI tools." This page shows the actual operating system: what I delegate, what I never delegate, and the receipts. No hype, no doom. Just how the work gets done in 2026.

TL;DR for hiring managers

AI compresses my execution time by roughly half. I reinvest that time into research depth, stakeholder alignment, and design decisions that AI can't make. The output isn't faster mediocrity. It's more time spent on the parts that determine whether a product wins.

The operating principle

A clear ledger: delegated vs. defended

Every task in my workflow sits on one side of this line. The line isn't about capability. It's about accountability. If a decision shapes user trust or business direction, it stays with me.

Delegated to AI Speed

  • First-pass interview transcription & coding Tagging 12 interviews took days. Now hours. I audit every tag.
  • Token systems to working prototypes Design tokens to interactive HTML in an afternoon, not a sprint.
  • Competitive scans & desk research Broad coverage fast. I verify sources and pull the signal myself.
  • Documentation drafts & spec boilerplate Handoff docs, state tables, edge-case checklists. I edit, AI drafts.
  • Variant exploration at volume 20 directions in the time one used to take. Taste picks the winner.

Defended as human Judgment

  • Problem framing & research questions Ask the wrong question and everything downstream is confidently wrong.
  • Live user conversations The insight is in the hesitation, the sigh, the workaround they're embarrassed about.
  • Design decisions & tradeoffs AI proposes. I decide, and I own the consequences in the retro.
  • Stakeholder alignment & facilitation Getting a room to agree is a contact sport. No model does it for you.
  • Ethical calls & dark-pattern refusals Someone has to say "we shouldn't." That someone has a name on the design.
The workflow

One loop, from question to shipped screen

This is the loop I ran on Onroute and run on every project since. Each step is labeled with who leads it.

Frame the problem

Define the job to be done, the riskiest assumption, and what evidence would change my mind. This framing is the highest-leverage 10% of the project.

Human

Desk research & competitive scan

AI does broad coverage across markets, patterns, and prior art. I interrogate the output, verify sources, and kill anything that smells like confident hallucination.

AI-led

Talk to users

Interviews and usability sessions, run live by me. The interview guide gets AI stress-testing beforehand: "what leading questions did I miss?"

Human

Synthesize

AI produces first-pass affinity clusters from transcripts. I re-sort, challenge the clusters, and write the insights. The interpretation is the deliverable, and it's mine.

Shared

Explore wide, decide narrow

Prompt-to-prototype lets me test 5 directions in the time one mockup used to take. Then judgment collapses the field: one direction, defended with evidence.

Shared

Craft, spec, ship

Final UI decisions, accessibility passes, and edge-state design by hand. AI drafts the handoff docs and state tables. Engineers get specs, not screenshots.
